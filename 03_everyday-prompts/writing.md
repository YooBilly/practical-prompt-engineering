# Writing Prompts

Practical prompts to help you write more clearly, faster, and with better structure.
These prompts are designed to be copied, adapted, and reused.

---

## 1. Clarify Before Writing

**Use when:** You don’t know where to start or the task feels vague.

    Before writing anything, ask me up to 5 clarifying questions about:
    - Audience
    - Goal
    - Tone
    - Length
    - Constraints

    Wait for my answers before writing.

---

## 2. Structure First, Write Second

**Use when:** You want clean, logical writing without rambling.

    Create a clear outline for the following topic:
    [TOPIC]

    Then write the content based strictly on the outline.
    Avoid adding new sections unless necessary.

---

## 3. Write for a Specific Reader

**Use when:** Your writing feels generic or unfocused.

    Write the following content as if you are speaking directly to:
    [A SPECIFIC PERSON OR ROLE]

    Assume they are intelligent but unfamiliar with the topic.
    Avoid unnecessary jargon.

---

## 4. Reduce Fluff

**Use when:** The text is too long or padded with filler.

    Rewrite the following text to be:
    - Clear
    - Direct
    - Free of fluff
    - No longer than necessary

    Preserve the original meaning.

---

## 5. Improve Clarity

**Use when:** Your message isn’t landing as intended.

    Rewrite the following text to maximize clarity.

    Guidelines:
    - Short sentences
    - Simple words
    - One idea per paragraph

---

## 6. Adjust Tone Without Changing Meaning

**Use when:** The tone feels wrong but the content is correct.

    Rewrite the following text in this tone:
    [FRIENDLY / PROFESSIONAL / CONFIDENT / NEUTRAL]

    Do not change the meaning or remove important details.

---

## 7. Explain Like I’m New

**Use when:** You are learning or teaching something unfamiliar.

    Explain the following concept as if I have no prior knowledge.

    Use simple language, short explanations, and concrete examples.

---

## 8. Summarize Without Losing Meaning

**Use when:** You need a shorter version of long content.

    Summarize the following text in:
    [X] bullet points.

    Preserve the core ideas and intent.

---

## 9. Generate Variations

**Use when:** You want multiple options to choose from.

    Generate 3 alternative versions of the following text.

    Each version should use different phrasing while preserving the same meaning.

---

## 10. Self-Critique and Improve

**Use when:** You want a higher-quality final result.

    Review the following text and identify:
    - Weak or unclear points
    - Missing context
    - Opportunities for improvement

    Then rewrite an improved version.
